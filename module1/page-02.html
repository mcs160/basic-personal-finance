What is a bank?

A bank is a business that sells financial services such as loans like car, home mortgage, and business. Accounts such as checking accounts or savings accounts and credit card services. Banks can keep money for saving or commercial purposes, and also for exchanging, investing, and supplying it for loans.

Where do banks get loan money?

Banks get their money from people who open savings and other types of accounts. Banks pool all this money to make loans.Banks act as the go between for people who deposit money into the bank and people who need to borrow money. Is my money safe in a bank?

The Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation, or FDIC, protects most types of deposits, including checking and savings, up to $100,000.
