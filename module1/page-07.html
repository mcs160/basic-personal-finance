Video Transcript

In Actions and Activities, you will learn about how to fill out and use deposit slips and checks. There will be a chance to practice what you learn after the video.

In this video, you will learn how to fill out a deposit slip and write a check, but first, a quick review. Deposit slips are used to put money into checking and savings accounts at your bank.Checks are promissory notes used to pay for goods and services, like food or car detailing, using money in your checking account.

The deposit slip allows you to deposit checks, cash/coins, and make withdrawals at a bank using a teller. When completing a deposit slip, select which account to deposit money into by marking the checking or savings box at the top of the slip. Next, write your ten-digit account number in the boxes provided. Print your first name, last name and address, but do not sign the deposit-slip yet. You must sign your first and last name in cursive on the slip in front of the bank teller for it to be valid. Next, print the date, the total amount of cash/coins you will deposit, the total amount of all the checks combined, and add together, or subtotal, the cash and check amounts. If you would like to withdraw any money, you will write in the amount in the Less Cash Rec’vd box. Next, subtract the Less Cash Rec'vd from the Subtotal to calculate your final total and write it in the bottom box. While not all deposit slips are exactly the same, this tutorial should help you know what to look for when filling out a deposit slip.

The check is a promise to pay for goods and services using money typically found in your checking account. Savings accounts may also have checks, but they are not used to pay for everyday items since there is usually a limit on the number of times money can be withdrawn from the account per month. When filling out a check, write the name of the person or company that you are promising to pay in the “pay to the order of” line. Next, spell-out the words of the amount you are promising to pay on the line below. For example, write the word ten and then 10/100 to indicate $10.10. You should also draw a line so no additional amount can be written in later. Next, print the date, using words and numbers or in an abbreviated form such as 12/24/16. Below, write the amount you are promising to pay in numerical form. And finally, in cursive, sign your first and last name on the line in the right hand corner. You may also chose to write what the check is for in the memo box, but this is not required.

We hope you have a better understanding of how to fill out a deposit slip and write a check. Let’s see what you have learned.
