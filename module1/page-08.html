Deposit Slips

The deposit slip allows you to deposit checks, cash/coins, and make withdrawals at a bank using a teller. While not all deposit slips are exactly the same, this tutorial will help you know what to look for when filling out a deposit slip. There will be an opportunity to practice what you learn in the next few screens. When completing the deposit slip, you must select which account you will be depositing money into by marking the checking or savings box at the top of the slip. Next, write your ten-digit account number in the boxes provided. Print your first and last name, address, but do not sign the deposit-slip yet. You must sign the slip in front of the bank teller for it to be valid. Next, print the date, the total amount of cash/coins you will be depositing, the total amount of all the checks combined, and subtotal the cash and check amounts. If you would like to withdraw any money, you will write in the amount in the Less Cash Rec'vd. Next, you will subtract the Less Cash Rec'vd from the Subtotal to calcuate your final total that you will write in the bottom box.
