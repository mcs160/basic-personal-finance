Banking 103.4: Review

In this module, you were introduced to banking. You learned the definition of bank, basic banking concepts, and money management skills. Play the video to review the material you learned in this module.

Banking review video will be placed here.

Below is the transcript:

In this module, you learned the basics of banking and personal finance. You learned the definition of a bank, which is a financial institution that accepts deposits of money from the public, maintains financial accounts for their customers, and creates loans and credit.

You should know the tools and services a bank offers to help customers manage money. A savings account is used for long-term saving and earns interest on the money you deposit. A checking account is used for day-to-day transactions and purchases.

You can use your ATM or debit card to withdrawal money from your savings and checking account at an ATM machine. You can also use checks or a debit card to make purchases or pay bills and withdrawal directly from your checking account.

Online banking is a great tool to manage your accounts, monitor account activity, and review bank statements.

Budgeting is a powerful financial management tool, and you should have a healthy balance between your income and your spending.
