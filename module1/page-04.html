Banking 102.1: Savings

Deposit Slip

When depositing money at a bank, fill out a deposit slip found at the bank or in the back of your checkbook. Your deposit slip must have the correct account number, date, and the correct currency. Cash and coins need to be separate from checks, and each check should be listed separately and endorsed. Calculate the total deposit, and indicate if you would like cash back.

A savings account is a bank account that earns interest on the money you have deposited. Banks pay interest on money you put into savings accounts because the bank is able to use your money to make loans to other customers. The amount of interest you will earn depends on the type of savings account you have, which bank has the account, and how long you keep your money in the account.
